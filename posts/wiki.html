A list of my favorite Wikipedia articles in no particular order. Some of these were popularized by Depths of Wikipedia, who curates an excellent collection of Wikipedia esoterica.

  • [01] List of lists of lists (LoLoL) - As the name suggests, it's a great starting place.
  • [02] Electrical distruptions caused by squirrels - In particular the part about NASDAQ outages.
  • [03] Operation Sea-Spray - The reason pink slime grows on shower drains in San Francisco.
  • [04] Saturn's hexagon - There is a hexgon at the north pole of saturn and nobody knows why.
  • [05] Piracy - The part on rewards addresses some cool misconceptions but the whole article goes hard.
  • [06] Cepstrum - And the related idea of quefrency alanysis (yes, that is the J.W. Tukey who did FFT).
  • [07] Bum Farto - They say "he was nicknamed Bum because he fetched the firefighters' coffee" but that doesn't explain much to me.
  • [08] Dunce hat (topology) - Something I often wear when studying topology.
  • [09] Tanner v. United States - Jury verdicts are final, even if the jurors are pounding pitchers of beer, smoking weed, and doing coke.
  • [10] List of common misconceptions - It's a little Reddit-coded but there's some fun stuff on here.
  • [11] Got the morbs - Victorian slang for feeling a temporary sadness that's ripe for revival.
  • [12] Sonic hedgehog protein - Yeah they really named it that.
  • [13] Insect repellent - A Victoria's Secret perfume is an effective insect repellent for 2 hours. Unclear how effective it is as a scent.
